ğŸŒ± Childhood and Early Life

ğŸ”¹Dr. Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in a modest Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam at a local mosque, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a homemaker. Despite facing financial struggles, young Kalam was a bright and hardworking student. He distributed newspapers to support his family while continuing his education.

ğŸ”¹Dr. Kalam was the youngest of five siblings, including three brothers and one sister. His elder brothers were Mohammed Muthu Meera Lebbai Maraikayar, Mustafa Kalam, and Kasim Mohammed, while his elder sister was Asim Zohra. He shared a close bond with his family, particularly with his eldest brother, Mohammed Muthu Meera Lebbai Maraikayar, who provided financial support for his education and encouraged his academic growth. Another key influence in his life was Ahmed Jalaluddin, a close family friend who was fifteen years older than Kalam. He later became Kalam's brother-in-law and played a significant role as a mentor. Jalaluddin, a well-read individual, introduced Kalam to literature, newspapers, and global affairs, nurturing his curiosity and inspiring his pursuit of knowledge. Both his elder brother and brother-in-law provided unwavering support, laying a strong foundation for Kalam's future achievements.

ğŸ”¹His cousin, Samsuddin, also played a significant role in his formative years by offering him his first job delivering newspapers during World War II to support the family's finances. Due to the suspension of train stoppages at Rameswaram station, newspaper bundles were thrown from moving trains, and Kalam assisted Samsuddin in collecting them. Samsuddin's decision to involve Kalam in his newspaper distribution was instrumental in shaping his work ethic and broadening his understanding of the world, as it exposed him to current events and the importance of information dissemination. This experience not only instilled a sense of responsibility and self-confidence in Dr. Kalam but also marked the beginning of his journey toward self-reliance.

ğŸ”¹The strong family support system, combined with his curiosity and interest in science and aeronautics shaped his future path. He was deeply influenced by his teachers and developed an early fascination with flight and engineering. His simple upbringing and values of humility, discipline, and hard work stayed with him throughout his life.

This period cemented his ambition to work on advanced aeronautics and space technologies, leading to his future role in shaping India's missile and space programs.
